Teaching

Since November 2022, I have served as a Lecturer at the School of Humanities and Social Sciences, University of Greenwich, where I teach a module on the “Political Economy of Extractivism: Global Perspectives on Mining and Ecologies” for the MSc program in Global Environmental Change. My responsibilities include preparing and delivering lectures, seminars, and workshops that critically analyze the political dimensions of climate change, resource extraction, and environmental justice. I also develop curriculum that incorporates interdisciplinary perspectives on sustainability and resource management. A key part of my role is designing assessments, such as essays, exams, and presentations, to evaluate student understanding. Additionally, I provide constructive feedback to support their academic growth.

 

Since November 2019, I have also worked as an Academic Tutor in International Development at the International Study Centre, University of Sussex. I design modules on themes like geopolitical theories, climate geopolitics, and resource management. My work includes creating instructional materials tailored for hybrid learning environments, including multimedia presentations and online discussion boards. I implement formative and summative assessments, such as quizzes and debates, to foster critical thinking about environmental issues like extractive mining. Furthermore, I offer personalized tutoring to support students in developing essays and projects related to environmental policy and resource conflicts.
