WELCOME!

I'M SAUMYA RANJAN NATH

I possess thirteen years of combined professional and research experience, including four years dedicated to teaching, supervising, curriculum development, seminar facilitation, grading, and mentoring students.

I completed my PhD in History (Environmental History/Political Ecology) at the School of Media, Arts, and Humanities, University of Sussex, Brighton, United Kingdom.MA in Social Work at Tata Institute of Social Sciences, Mumbai, India & B.A (Honours) in English Literature at Utkal University, Bhubaneswar, India

My doctoral dissertation, Defending the Sacred: Discourses of Development, Identity, and Everyday Resistance among the Dongria Kondhs in Eastern India,, interrogates the environmental historiography and socio-political anthropology of the Dongria Kondhs, foregrounding the dialectics of marginalization, dispossession, and subaltern resistance as legacies of colonial and postcolonial hegemonies. My professional experience spans interdisciplinary research, policy advocacy, and sustainable development, including leading a CHASE Doctoral Training Partnership project to assess postgraduate researcher development across UK universities, contributing to global income inequality studies at the University of Sussex, and conducting data analysis with R and Python. I have designed policy frameworks for mining, forest rights, and environmental governance in India, facilitated solar electrification and cultural preservation initiatives for indigenous communities, and collaborated on ecological restoration and disaster management plans with national and international stakeholders, contributing to sustainable livelihoods and social protection programs.
