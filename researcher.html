Research

I contributed to two significant projects led by Professor Vinita Damodaran, highlighting the intersection of academia and socio-environmental activism. As part of the AHRC-funded Academia and Activism initiative, I organized workshops, engaged with early career researchers, and helped develop frameworks bridging academic research with activist praxis to address global challenges. Additionally, for the British Academy-funded Botany, Climate, and Empire project, I analyzed archival materials to uncover the ecological and sociopolitical legacies of colonial environmental practices, exploring the interplay between botanical knowledge, climate change, and imperial exploitation. Together, these projects fostered interdisciplinary dialogues and emphasized the transformative potential of knowledge co-creation.
